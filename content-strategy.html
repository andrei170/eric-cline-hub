Phase 3

Content Strategy · Eric Cline

Prepared 2026-05-13 by AG1 Consulting. Eric's content engine is already strong and multi-channel, IG, YouTube, TikTok, the REFUSE TO LOSE podcast. This doc is not about making more content. It is about the one channel that is missing for his actual buyer (LinkedIn) and about pointing the content he already makes at the funnel that now exists. See funnel-blueprint for the architecture and bottleneck-analysis for the revenue math.

The brief

Two jobs: 1. Build the missing distribution channel. LinkedIn, 3-4x/week, ghostwritten in Eric's voice, for the aspiring-wholesaler ICP that does its due diligence there before buying a five-figure program. This is the recurring engagement (Bottleneck #5). 2. Re-point the content Eric already makes. Every IG bio, YouTube description, TikTok profile, and podcast show note currently points at a scattered funnel. Once the hub exists, they all point at one front door. This is a settings change, not a content change, but it is the highest-leverage move in the doc.

Job 1 · The LinkedIn Distribution Engine (recurring engagement)

Why LinkedIn, why this ICP

Eric's audience is concentrated on Instagram, with YouTube, TikTok, and the podcast as secondary channels. A real slice of his ideal buyer is not on Instagram for buying decisions. The W-2 professional weighing whether to leave their job for virtual wholesaling, the aspiring wholesaler doing due diligence on a five-figure coaching commitment, the investor-curious operator, those people research on LinkedIn. A $4.5M-exit operator with a decade in virtual sales is exactly the profile LinkedIn rewards, and right now that credibility is invisible there.

There is also a defensive reason. There is a public BiggerPockets thread asking "is Eric Cline's wholesale course worth it?" When a prospect is doing due diligence, a strong LinkedIn presence with the operator story laid out plainly is the single best counterweight to a "is this worth it?" forum thread.

Cadence

3-4x/week, ghostwritten in Eric's aggressive, results-led, operator-direct voice from his existing knowledge. No new methodology, every post comes from IP that already exists in his head, his paid products, and his podcast.

Content pillars

  1. The operator story, told in pieces. The $250M-over-the-phone story, the $2.6M first year, the $11M over four years, the $4.5M exit, all attributed to Eric as his story, broken into individual posts. This is the trust spine.
  2. The 5-step sales process, taught in public. Pieces of Eric's actual process, the cold-call open, the objection rebuttals, the follow-up discipline. Give the framework away; the package is the implementation.
  3. Virtual-wholesaling deal breakdowns. "Here is a deal I closed without ever seeing the property." Concrete, number-dense, proof-through-detail. The phone-only moat made visible.
  4. "Is wholesaling worth it" myth-busting. Aimed directly at the due-diligence searcher. Honest, outcome-realistic, regulated-niche-aware (wholesaling legality varies by state, results are not typical). This pillar is the counterweight to the forum thread, and it doubles as compliance-aware positioning.
  5. The W-2-to-wholesaler transition. Speaking to the professional who has not jumped yet, the doubt, the math, the realistic timeline. This is the top-of-funnel pillar for the LinkedIn ICP.

Starter hook bank (LinkedIn, Eric's voice)

  • "I closed $250M in real estate without ever walking a property. Here is the first thing I'd tell anyone starting today."
  • "Everyone wants the deal. Almost nobody wants the part of the call that gets you the deal. Here is the part."
  • "I sold my wholesale operation for seven figures in 2024. The skill that built it was not finding houses."
  • "Wholesaling is regulated differently in different states. Before you do anything, here is what nobody on Instagram tells you."
  • "The W-2 to wholesaler jump is not a leap of faith. It is a math problem. Here is the math."
  • "Three objections kill more wholesale deals than bad leads ever will. Here is how I answer all three."
  • "A 15-year veteran joined my program and hit his first consistent $100K month. He did not need new leads. He needed the call."

All hooks route to the hub. Every post ends with the hub as the destination, not a scattered funnel page.

Job 2 · Re-Point The Existing Content Engine

This is the fast, high-leverage half of the doc. Eric already publishes constantly. The content is fine. The destinations are broken.

Channel Current destination New destination
IG bio link One go.theericcline.com page The hub
YouTube video descriptions Scattered / inconsistent The hub, with a free-lead-magnet CTA
TikTok profile link One page The hub
Podcast show notes No funnel The hub, with a podcast-listener opt-in CTA (Bottleneck #4)
LinkedIn profile link Underweight / inconsistent The hub

Once the hub exists, this is a settings change across five channels. It costs almost nothing and it is the single change that makes every other fix in the package work, because all the routing finally lands somewhere coherent.

How The Free Lead Magnet Gets Promoted

The "Virtual Wholesaler's First-Call Script + 5-Step Sales Process Cheat Sheet" is the entry point. It gets promoted, not as a one-time launch, but as the standing CTA across the content engine:

  • IG: in-feed posts and reels referencing "the free script, link in bio"
  • YouTube: every video description, plus pinned-comment CTA
  • TikTok: profile link + in-video reference
  • Podcast: show-notes CTA on every episode, plus an in-episode mention (Bottleneck #4)
  • LinkedIn: woven into the 5-step-process and deal-breakdown pillars

The hook is always the concrete tool, "the actual first-call script, free", never the aspiration. Eric's audience responds to the tool and the proof.

Year-2 Stretch (parked)

  • YouTube as a funnel channel, not just a content channel. Eric's YouTube is already active. A Year-2 layer wires it into the funnel more deliberately, dedicated funnel-entry videos, end-screen routing to the hub, a YouTube-specific lead magnet variant.
  • Repurposing the podcast into LinkedIn-native clips. Once the LinkedIn engine has traction, the REFUSE TO LOSE back catalogue becomes a content mine for LinkedIn-native posts.

Both parked behind the five core moves. Noted here so they are on the roadmap, not in the Year-1 floor math.
