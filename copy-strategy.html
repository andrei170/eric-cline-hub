Copy Strategy · Eric Cline

Foundation doc. Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, the framing AG1 writes to across every surface in the package. Every copy decision on the hub, the landing page, the emails, and the VSL maps back to this doc.

Voice

Aggressive, high-energy, results-and-numbers-led, blue-collar-grit, competitive. Eric's register is built from one credential carrying everything: he did it at scale before he taught it. His own stated $250M+ in sales over the telephone, $2.6M in year one, $11M over four years, a reported $4.5M exit in 2024. That operator story is the trust spine, and the voice should carry it the way Eric carries it, loud, confident, proof-first, never apologetic. He talks like a phone closer because he is one. Short, punchy, declarative sentences. Numbers everywhere. The audience is aspiring and active wholesalers, mostly operators who want to be the top closer in the room, and Eric talks to them operator-to-operator at full volume, not coach-to-student in a soft register.

Use: REFUSE TO LOSE, GOAT, King, #1, 7-figure, virtual, the phone, assignment fee, the 5-step sales process, close, closer, deals, wholesaler, remote, run it remote, winners, the game, scale, elite, the numbers, on the phone, deal flow, the script, objections, rebuttals, follow up, did it then taught it

Avoid (AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies): the standard AG1 banned-phrase list, see the obfuscation note below. Plus, specific to Eric: soft-feminine / elegance-led cadence, measured / concierge register, "journey," "transform your life," "unlock your potential," anything that lowers the energy below Eric's baseline or makes him sound like a gentle mentor instead of a competitive operator. Preserve Eric's actual brand words, GOAT, King, REFUSE TO LOSE, 7-figure, elite, those are his real vocabulary, not banned slop, and stripping them would make the copy read off-brand.

Banned-phrase handling: The AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies to every file in this package. In the non-copy-strategy deliverables, banned phrases are obfuscated rather than spelled out, the instruction is "the AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies, see copy-strategy.md." This file is the single place the constraint is named. The list covers the usual AI-slop and hype-cliche set (the "game-changer / revolutionary / unlock / seamless / effortless / paradigm" family and the rest of the standard AG1 list). Eric's genuine brand vocabulary is explicitly exempt.

Brand Pillars

  1. Did it, then taught it. Eric's authority is not theoretical. He built and exited a virtual wholesale operation before he coached anyone. Every piece of copy leans on the operator story as the proof, not on credentials or claims of being the best.
  2. The phone is the moat. Eric's whole positioning is virtual / phone-only wholesaling, "King of Virtual Wholesale RE." The copy keeps that moat front and center. He is not another house-tour wholesaler; he closes deals over the telephone, and so can his students.
  3. The numbers run first. Eric's content and proof are number-dense by nature, $250M, $2.6M, $11M, $4.5M, 5-step process, 7-figure. The copy matches that density. Specific figures, not vague aspiration, attributed to Eric where they are his claims.
  4. The system is the product, the architecture is the gap. Eric already has the offers, the course, GOAT Gang, ELITE, DealCon. The package is not inventing a product; it is connecting the system. The copy frames every fix as "you built the offers, this connects them," never as "you have nothing."

Framing For The Funnel

  • Top of funnel (IG hooks, the free lead magnet, YouTube descriptions, podcast show notes, LinkedIn opening lines): mechanism-led and proof-led. The free first-call-script lead magnet is the entry point, the hook is "the actual script, free," not "make money wholesaling." Eric's audience responds to the concrete tool and the operator proof, not the aspiration.
  • Mid funnel (the hub, the free lead magnet opt-in, the legible ladder section): the operator story plus the legible-ladder framing. Specific numbers, attributed to Eric. The ladder section makes every tier legible, who it is for, what the commitment is, hinted not dropped on the coaching prices, per the D100 hint-don't-drop standard.
  • High funnel (GOAT Gang, ELITE, DealCon): for the buyer who has run the free script, bought the $997 course, and wants the live coaching. Sold by the legible ladder and the VSL, the buyer can see exactly where they are and what the next rung is. The copy never hides the path; it just hints at the price the way Eric already does.

Compliance Constraints AG1 Writes To

Real estate wholesaling is a regulated, FTC-watched niche on two axes, income claims and state-level legality. Every customer-facing surface in this package writes to these constraints:

  • Income claims stay outcome-honest. The framework is teachable; the result is not promised. No "you will make $X." Results-not-typical language in the landing footer. Eric's own numbers are attributed to Eric, not stated as what a student will get.
  • Wholesaling legality varies by state. The landing footer names that real estate wholesaling is regulated and restricted in several states and that some states require a real estate license. The copy never implies wholesaling is universally legal or licence-free.
  • Educator, not licensed advisor. Eric is positioned as a sales coach and educator, not a licensed broker advising on a specific transaction, not an attorney, not a financial advisor. The copy holds that line.
  • No guarantee language. The DIY course explicitly has no refund policy. The copy writes no guarantee or refund promises into any tier unless Eric confirms otherwise on Day 1.
  • Attribution discipline. $250M, $2.6M, $11M, $4.5M exit, the $50K-deal and $100K-month student references, all attributed to Eric as his claims ("Eric has done...", "Eric reports..."), never stated as AG1-verified independent fact.
  • Real testimonials only. Only the two named GOAT Gang testimonials (Jason Corley, Danny Sanchez), and only with Eric's Day-1 confirmation. The receipts blocks use HTML-comment placeholders otherwise. Nothing invented.
