Bottleneck Analysis · Eric Cline

Prepared 2026-05-13 by AG1 Consulting. Five bottlenecks, ranked by revenue impact. Year-1 math calibrated to the 30K-150K IG tier (~110K followers). Each bottleneck carries two numbers: a conservative floor and a realistic target with full execution. See research for the source audit and funnel-blueprint for the architecture these fixes install.

How to read this

Each bottleneck below carries: a Snapshot (what is true today), the Asymmetry (the gap between what Eric has and what he captures), the Cost of Waiting (conservative Year-1 floor leaking right now), the Fix (what gets built), the Impact (Year-1 floor and target once installed), and Why this is #N (the ranking logic).

The floor numbers are conservative. They are calibrated to a ~110K Instagram audience in the real estate wholesaling education niche, with an existing paid ladder already converting, and they assume low-end conversion, no audience growth, and no compounding. At this tier the honest floor math is meaningful per bottleneck and significant combined.

Combined Year-1 floor across the five core bottlenecks: $180K-$470K, conservative. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution: $380K-$1.05M.

The key framing for this build: Eric is not a zero-offer prospect. He has a $997 course, a Skool community (GOAT Gang), a premium coaching tier (ELITE), an event (DealCon), and a real multi-channel audience. The problem is not absence. It is architecture, the offers were built as separate funnel pages instead of one connected system, and the connective layer (a hub, an email-gated free asset, a legible ladder, a podcast funnel) is missing.

Floor vs. target · the calibration logic

The floor is the honest worst-acceptable case. The install lands, but conversion sits at the low end of every range, the email list barely grows, the LinkedIn engine posts without compounding much, and the ~110K Instagram audience plus the YouTube, TikTok, and podcast channels stay roughly flat across the year. That is the $180K-$470K number, and it is deliberately the figure a skeptical reader can defend on a bad day.

The target is what Year-1 looks like when the install lands well and three compounding effects all show up, none of them speculative:

  1. The recurring-engagement bottleneck (#5) compounds. A 3-to-4x/week LinkedIn cadence does not pay flat across 12 months. It builds. The post archive, the follower base, and the inbound from the aspiring-wholesaler ICP all grow month over month, so month 10 outperforms month 2. The floor treats #5 as roughly flat; the target lets the back half of the year carry its real weight, which roughly doubles #5 from $40K-$120K to $95K-$270K.
  2. Audience growth feeds the funnel. The distribution bottleneck (#5) does not just sell, it grows the audience, and Eric already has an active multi-channel content engine (IG, YouTube, TikTok, podcast) that keeps adding reach. Every new follower and every new LinkedIn connection is a new top-of-funnel entry into the lead magnet and the legible ladder. The floor assumes a static ~110K; the target assumes the realistic audience growth a multi-channel operator with a new LinkedIn engine actually sees in a year.
  3. The owned email list compounds on itself. The floor runs the whole year as if the lead magnet captures one batch and the ladder converts it once at the low end. In reality the list grows every month the lead magnet is promoted across IG, YouTube, TikTok, and the podcast, the nurture sequence keeps converting subscribers well past the first opt-in, and the podcast funnel keeps feeding it every episode. An owned list in month 12 is larger and warmer than the same list in month 2.

Per-bottleneck, floor to target: #1 hub $36K-$108K to $75K-$220K (a year of compounding capture as the established front door instead of a standing start), #2 lead magnet $40K-$110K to $90K-$240K (the list grows all year across every channel and the nurture keeps converting), #3 legible ladder $30K-$84K to $60K-$170K (more course buyers climb as the ladder gets refined and the rungs above fill), #4 podcast funnel $30K-$84K to $60K-$170K (every episode across the year feeds the funnel instead of the funnel catching one cohort), #5 LinkedIn $40K-$120K to $95K-$270K (compounding cadence plus the audience growth it drives). The target is roughly 2.2x the top of the floor, and it is anchored in those five lines of math, not a blind multiplier. If a reader asks "how do you get to $1.05M," this is the answer: the floor with the compounding the floor deliberately leaves out.

Bottleneck #1 · No Unified Hub Connecting The Scattered Funnel

Impact tier: Load-Bearing Infrastructure

Snapshot

theericcline.com is a navigation shell. The actual offers live as separate, standalone pages on the go.theericcline.com subdomain, the $997 course on one page, GOAT Gang on another, ELITE on another, an A-Z Bootcamp on the root, an "info" gateway on another, a "sales-page" on another. Verified from direct fetches: there is no single destination that explains the whole ladder, tells a cold visitor who Eric is, and shows them what to do next at every commitment level.

The Asymmetry

A ~110K IG audience, a podcast, a YouTube channel, a Skool community, and a $4.5M-exit operator story, and there is no front door. A cold visitor, someone who heard Eric on a podcast, got referred by a GOAT Gang member, or found a clip, lands on whichever single funnel page the link happens to point at. They see one offer, out of context, with no map of the rest. The IG bio link can only point at one page, so it picks a winner and orphans everything else.

Every other bottleneck on this list needs a destination. The lead magnet needs a page. The podcast funnel needs somewhere to send listeners. The legible ladder needs one surface to live on. Without the hub, the other four fixes have nowhere to route.

Cost of Waiting

A scattered funnel converts worse than a connected one at every tier, because cold traffic that lands mid-funnel with no context bounces. Conservatively, the missing hub costs ~$3-9K/mo in course sales and coaching applications that never happen because the visitor never saw the whole picture. Across 12 months that is ~$36K-$108K leaking, and it compounds, because every month of audience growth arrives into the same disconnected scatter.

6 months from now: ~$18-54K never collected, plus six months of podcast episodes, YouTube uploads, and IG growth all pointing at a funnel with no front door.

The Fix

One standalone audience hub: hero section with a short VSL, the free lead magnet opt-in above the fold, an about + operator-story credibility section, the full offer ladder laid out clearly in one place, a real-testimonial receipts block (sourced from Eric on Day 1), an FAQ, and the A5 wholesaling compliance disclaimer footer. The IG bio link, the YouTube channel link, the podcast show notes, and the LinkedIn profile link all re-point here. Ships in less than 7 days.

Impact

Year-1 floor $36K-$108K direct. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution $75K-$220K from a year of compounding capture as the established front door instead of a standing start. Plus it is load-bearing for #2, #3, #4, and #5, none of them have anywhere to send traffic without it.

Why this is #1

It is not always the single highest direct floor, but nothing else on the list works without it. The lead magnet needs a landing surface. The legible ladder needs one home. The podcast funnel needs a destination. The LinkedIn engine needs somewhere to point. This is the foundation the other four stand on, so it ships first.

Bottleneck #2 · No Email-Gated Free Lead Magnet Building An Owned List

Impact tier: Audience-Capture · Compounding

Snapshot

Every funnel page fetched was a paid sales page or a checkout. The one free-adjacent asset surfaced, a "Script Download," appears to be an ungated direct download, not an email-gated list-builder. Verified finding: there is no observable free asset that converts a follower into an email subscriber Eric owns.

The Asymmetry

Eric has a ~110K Instagram audience, plus YouTube, TikTok, and a podcast. That is a large rented audience, rented in the sense that the platforms own the relationship, not Eric. The email list is the only audience asset no algorithm can take away, and right now it is barely being built, because there is no compelling free asset that gives a follower a concrete reason to hand over an email address.

Eric has exactly the material that makes a great lead magnet sitting in his head and in his paid products, the cold-call script, the 5-step sales process, the objection rebuttals. A free, professionally packaged slice of that, the "Virtual Wholesaler's First-Call Script + 5-Step Sales Process Cheat Sheet," email-gated, would convert followers into a list at scale. None of it is packaged that way today.

Cost of Waiting

A free lead magnet promoted to a ~110K IG audience plus YouTube, TikTok, and podcast traffic could conservatively add thousands of email subscribers in Year-1. At a conservative downstream conversion into the existing ladder (the $997 course, GOAT Gang, ELITE), that list is worth ~$40K-$110K Year-1 that is currently not being built at all. Every month without the lead magnet is a month of audience growth that never becomes an owned, re-marketable asset.

6 months from now: ~$20-55K of list value never built, plus the nurture sequence that would have warmed those subscribers toward the course and GOAT Gang never ran.

The Fix

One flagship lead magnet, the "Virtual Wholesaler's First-Call Script + 5-Step Sales Process Cheat Sheet" (or similar, locked with Eric on Day 1), professionally designed, email-gated on the hub. Backed by a 5-email nurture sequence (built, see email-sequence) that warms subscribers from the free script toward the $997 course and GOAT Gang. Promoted from the IG bio link, YouTube descriptions, TikTok, and podcast show notes.

Impact

Year-1 floor $40K-$110K, compounding, because the list keeps growing and keeps converting after Year-1. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution $90K-$240K as the list grows all year across every channel and the nurture keeps converting well past the first opt-in.

Why this is #2

It is the engine that fills the existing ladder. The course and GOAT Gang already convert, they are just running on bio-link clicks and ad traffic alone. The lead magnet turns the ~110K rented audience into an owned, nurturable list that feeds every paid tier. It ranks below the hub only because the lead magnet needs the hub to live on, but it is the highest compounding floor on the list.

Bottleneck #3 · No Legible Mid-Tier Step In The Ladder

Impact tier: Conversion Architecture

Snapshot

The ladder, as a cold visitor experiences it, runs: $997 DIY course, then GOAT Gang (price hidden, collected at checkout), then ELITE (price hidden, collected at checkout). The two coaching tiers hide their commitment level entirely. There is no obvious, legible step between "buy the $997 course" and "apply for live coaching at an unknown price."

The Asymmetry

A buyer who finishes the $997 course and is ready for more hits a wall. The next step up, GOAT Gang, does not show its price or its exact commitment until they click into a checkout. The same is true of ELITE. A buyer cannot self-qualify, cannot see where they fit, cannot picture the path. Hidden pricing is a normal high-ticket tactic for the top tier, but when every tier above the entry course hides its price, the ladder stops functioning as a ladder. It becomes two opaque doors.

The fix is not necessarily to publish every price, the D100 standard is to hint, not drop. The fix is to make the ladder legible: name each tier, describe who it is for, describe the commitment level in plain language, and give each one a clear "this is your next step" so the buyer can place themselves on the ladder without a checkout-page click.

Cost of Waiting

Course buyers who would have climbed to GOAT Gang or ELITE but could not see the path, conservatively ~$2.5-7K/mo in mid-tier and high-tier conversions that stall at the opaque step. Across 12 months, ~$30K-$84K that leaks because the ladder is not legible.

6 months from now: ~$15-42K never collected, plus every course buyer in that window who finished, was ready, and quietly drifted because they could not see where to go next.

The Fix

A legible ladder section on the hub and landing page: every tier named (free script, $997 course, GOAT Gang, ELITE, DealCon), each with a one-line "who this is for," each with a plain-language commitment description and a clear next-step link. Hint at the tier shape without dropping the exact coaching prices, per the D100 hint-don't-drop standard. The buyer can see the whole climb and place themselves on it.

Impact

Year-1 floor $30K-$84K direct, by recovering mid-tier and high-tier conversions that currently stall at the opaque step. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution $60K-$170K as more course buyers climb once the ladder is refined and the rungs above it fill.

Why this is #3

It is pure conversion architecture, no new offer, no new audience, just making the existing ladder legible so buyers can climb it. It ranks below the hub and the lead magnet because it needs the hub to live on and the lead magnet to feed the bottom of the ladder, but it is a fast, high-leverage fix on revenue that is already half-earned.

Bottleneck #4 · No Nurture Funnel For The REFUSE TO LOSE Podcast Audience

Impact tier: Warm-Audience-Capture · Compounding

Snapshot

Eric hosts the REFUSE TO LOSE podcast, an active asset with deal-story episodes. No funnel was surfaced routing podcast listeners anywhere, not into the free lead magnet, not into the email list, not into the paid ladder.

The Asymmetry

Podcast listeners are the warmest, highest-context segment of any creator's audience. They have chosen to spend 30-to-60-minute blocks with Eric, in his voice, hearing his deal stories and sales philosophy in depth. They are pre-sold on the operator. And right now there is no observable path from "finished the episode" to "joined the list" to "bought the course." The warmest segment in the stack is currently unmonetized as a funnel.

This is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-friction installs available, because the audience already exists and is already warm. It just needs a route.

Cost of Waiting

A podcast-to-funnel route, a consistent show-notes CTA into the free lead magnet, a dedicated podcast-listener opt-in page, and a segmented welcome sequence, conservatively captures ~$2.5-7K/mo in course and GOAT Gang conversions from listeners who are currently hearing the whole pitch and then hitting a dead end. Across 12 months, ~$30K-$84K that leaks while the podcast keeps publishing.

6 months from now: ~$15-42K never collected, plus every episode published in that window that warmed a listener and then sent them nowhere.

The Fix

A podcast nurture funnel: a consistent show-notes and in-episode CTA into the free lead magnet, a dedicated podcast-listener opt-in path on the hub, and a short segmented welcome sequence that meets the listener where they are (already warm, already pre-sold on Eric) and routes them toward the $997 course and GOAT Gang. All built on the hub and the email infrastructure from #1 and #2.

Impact

Year-1 floor $30K-$84K, compounding, because the podcast keeps publishing and the funnel keeps catching listeners after Year-1. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution $60K-$170K as every episode released across the year feeds the funnel instead of the funnel catching a single cohort.

Why this is #4

It is warm-audience capture, the highest-leverage kind, but it ranks below the hub, the lead magnet, and the legible ladder because it routes into all three. The podcast funnel needs the hub to point at, the lead magnet to offer, and the ladder to climb. Once those exist, this is one of the fastest wins in the package.

Bottleneck #5 · No LinkedIn Distribution For The Investor-Curious / Aspiring-Wholesaler ICP

Impact tier: Recurring Engagement · Distribution Compound

Snapshot

Eric's distribution is concentrated on Instagram, with YouTube, TikTok, and the podcast as secondary channels. His LinkedIn profile exists but appears underweight from public surfaces, no visible posting cadence (LinkedIn returned a 999 anti-bot status at fetch time, so this is "appears underweight, confirm at install").

The Asymmetry

A real slice of Eric's ideal audience does not make buying decisions on Instagram. The aspiring wholesaler weighing a five-figure coaching commitment, the W-2 professional researching whether virtual wholesaling is real before they spend money, the investor-curious operator doing due diligence, those people research on LinkedIn before they ever DM a coach. A $4.5M-exit operator with a decade in virtual sales is exactly the profile LinkedIn rewards, and that credibility is currently invisible on the one channel where the highest-intent, highest-budget slice of his ICP is screening.

There is also the BiggerPockets-thread dynamic, when a prospect is doing due diligence on a coach, a strong LinkedIn presence with the operator story laid out is the single best counterweight to a "is this worth it?" forum thread. Right now that counterweight does not exist.

Cost of Waiting

A LinkedIn presence grown over 12 months, posting 3-4x/week in Eric's voice from his existing operator knowledge, conservatively drives ~$40K-$120K/yr in course sales, GOAT Gang and ELITE applications, and DealCon ticket sales that are currently never captured because that ICP cannot find Eric where they search and screen. Pre-call trust that does not compound is a conversion penalty.

12 months from now: $40-120K+ in compounding distribution never collected, plus 12 months of LinkedIn audience that never got built on the channel where the aspiring-wholesaler ICP actually does its due diligence.

The Fix

A monthly recurring engagement: LinkedIn 3-4x/week, ghostwritten in Eric's aggressive, results-led, operator-direct voice from his existing knowledge, virtual-wholesaling deal breakdowns, the 5-step sales process taught in public, phone-sales psychology, the $4.5M-exit operator story told in pieces, "is wholesaling worth it" myth-busting for the due-diligence searcher. All from IP that already exists in his head and his paid products, no new methodology required.

Impact

Year-1 floor $40K-$120K, on top of the Install bottlenecks, compounding every month. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution $95K-$270K as the cadence compounds across the year instead of paying flat, and grows the audience that feeds every other bottleneck.

Combined Install (#1, #2, #3, #4) + Recurring Engagement (#5) Year-1 floor: $180K-$470K, conservative at the ~110K audience tier. Realistic Year-1 target with full execution: $380K-$1.05M.

Why this is #5

It is a recurring engagement, not a one-time install, and its returns compound over time rather than landing on Day 7. It ranks last because the Install bottlenecks have to exist first, the LinkedIn engine needs the hub, the lead magnet, and the legible ladder to route traffic into. But it is the bottleneck that keeps the floor rising in Year-2 and reaches the part of the ICP no other channel does.
