Email Sequence · Eric Cline

Prepared 2026-05-13 by AG1 Consulting. The 5-email nurture sequence that runs after the free lead magnet opt-in, warming a subscriber from the free first-call script toward the $997 course and GOAT Gang. This is the sequence that turns the email list (Bottleneck #2) into revenue. Written in Eric's voice, see copy-strategy. The AG1 universal banned-phrase list applies throughout.

Where this sits

A new subscriber opts in for the "Virtual Wholesaler's First-Call Script + 5-Step Sales Process Cheat Sheet" on the hub. Email 1 delivers it immediately. Emails 2-5 run over the following days, teaching from the free asset and routing toward the ladder. Podcast-sourced subscribers (Bottleneck #4) get the same sequence with a one-line opener acknowledging the podcast, noted at the end of this doc.

Compliance: every email keeps income claims outcome-honest, attributes Eric's numbers to Eric, and never promises a result. The framework is teachable; the outcome is not promised. Wholesaling legality varies by state, the sequence references this where relevant rather than burying it.

The script you just downloaded is the exact open I use. The cheat sheet is the 5-step process the whole thing runs on.

Read the script today. Tomorrow I'll send you the part most people get wrong on step one.

Run it.

Email 3 · The objection (Day 3)

Subject: "I'm not interested" is not a no

Body:

Most wholesalers hear "I'm not interested" and hang up. That's a deal walking out the door.

"I'm not interested" early in a call is almost never a real no. It's a reflex. The seller is screening, not deciding. The 5-step cheat sheet you downloaded has the framework for getting past the reflex without being pushy about it.

Here's what I want you to understand. Objections are not the obstacle. Objections are the call. The closers who scale are the ones who got good at the part everyone else avoids.

That's a big piece of what's inside the course, and it's the entire reason GOAT Gang exists, live coaching, bi-monthly calls with me, a room of operators all working the same process, the document vault with every contract and script. The course teaches it. The Gang is where you get it coached.

Notes for the install

  • All [hub link] placeholders resolve to the live hub URL on Day 1. The ladder-section and course-tier anchors point at the relevant section of the hub.
  • Subject lines are written in Eric's punchy, declarative register. Test them; Eric's audience may respond to even more aggressive subject lines, that is a Day-1 calibration with Eric.
  • The sequence is deliberately 5 emails over 7 days. It can extend if Eric wants a longer nurture, but the floor math in bottleneck-analysis assumes this 5-email version.
  • Compliance is baked in, not bolted on. Email 4 carries the explicit results-not-typical and state-legality language so the sequence is covered even if a subscriber reads only one email.
